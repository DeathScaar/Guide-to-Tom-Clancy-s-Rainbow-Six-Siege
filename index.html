Guide to Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Siege

Tom Clancy’s Rainbow Six Siege is a tactical shooter pitting 5 players defending an objective against 5 other players, attacking the objective.

Features:

Rappelling:

Rappelling is a feature in Rainbow that allows attackers to grapple onto a roof and climb up a wall. Attackers can then climb onto the roof or breach into a window, launching them inside.

Destructible Environment:

In Rainbow, the environment, that is, the walls, hatches, and even reinforcements and barricades are mostly destructible. Walls and hatches that are not reinforced can be broken by breach charges and soft breachers such as ash, zofia, buck, and sledge.

Reinforcing:

Reinforcing is a very common feature inside the game which allows defenders to place metal coverings on destructible walls and hatches. This makes it so no one can shoot through the wall and only hard breachers such as thermite, hibana, maverick, and ace can break the wall.

Cameras:

Cameras in Rainbow adopt many forms. There are default map cameras which are accessible only by defenders, drones for attackers which allow for intel before breach into the objective. Operators such as valkyrie have special cameras. In her case, she has small cameras that can be thrown anywhere.

Team Killing:

srchough it can sometimes be annoying, team killing is a feature in the game that enables both attackers and defenders to damage and even kill their teammates.

Leaning:

Leaning in the game of Rainbow allows players to tilt their guns to either the left or right in order to allow for holding angles in gunfights.

Gadgets:

Every operator in Rainbow has a special gadget, from spike traps to body shields!

Game Modes:

Hostage

In this game mode, the defenders have to defend a hostage and the attackers have to either kill all defenders or escort the hostage outside safely.

Secure Area

This game mode consists of a biohazard container that has to be secured by the attacking side. This is done by securing the area in which the biohazard container is in.

Bomb

In the Bomb game mode, attackers have to plant a defuser near a bomb in order to win. If the attackers plant the defuser, the only way for the defense to win is to disable the defuser.

Newcomer

This game mode is intended for players new to siege, a place for them to learn the basics of the game.

Quick Match

Quick match is a non-competitive game mode that has no effect on players’ ranks.

Unranked

Unranked is meant for players that want to play competitively but without a consequence to their rank.

Ranked

Ranked is a competitive game mode, much like unranked except here, you get a rank and get matched with harder opponents as your rank progresses.

Training Grounds

Originally called Terrorist Hunt, this game mode allows players to practice their aim against bots. This game mode can consist of all the existing objectives, as well as elimination, in which your only objective is to eliminate the opposing bots.

Custom

In this game mode, you are able to choose the map you play, what objective you play, and can customize settings to your liking. This game mode is mostly used for battling a friend or setting up battles with multiple friends.

Operators:

Thermite

Thermite is a hard breach character who can blow up reinforced walls with his hard breaches to create another entry point to the objective site. His primary counters are Bandit, who can electrify walls and destroy his gadget, and Mute.

Mute

Mute is an operator who can block signals, stopping drones and hard breacheds, like Thermite’s.

Competitions:

Competitions

Rainbow is a game like many others that has open competitions with monetary rewards. Some of the best players include Pengu, Fabian, Canadian, and Goga.
